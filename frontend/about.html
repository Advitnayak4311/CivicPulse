About CivicPulse

Empowering citizens. Accelerating municipal response. Building smarter cities.

Our Mission

CivicPulse was built with a single, unwavering goal: to eliminate the gap between citizen-reported civic problems and timely government action.

Every day, millions of Indians navigate potholed roads, burst water mains, overflowing drains, and broken street lights — problems that persist for weeks or months due to inefficient reporting channels, opaque bureaucracy, and lack of accountability. CivicPulse replaces informal phone calls and paper petitions with a structured, transparent, and GPS-precise digital platform.

We believe that every citizen deserves a clean, safe, and functional urban environment — and that the path to achieving this starts with empowering people to be heard.

What CivicPulse Does

GPS-Precise Reporting

Citizens pin exact GPS coordinates on an interactive map, eliminating ambiguity. Photographic evidence is uploaded directly within the report.

Smart Department Routing

Our rule-based engine automatically assigns each complaint to the correct municipal department based on issue category — no manual triage required.

Live Lifecycle Tracking

Every status change — from Submitted to Resolved — is recorded in a tamper-evident timeline. Citizens track progress in real time without making a single phone call.

Emergency Fast-Track

Critical issues are escalated instantly through an OTP-verified emergency channel — no account required, highest priority automatically assigned.

Community Support Voting

Citizens experiencing the same issue can "support" an existing complaint, boosting its priority score and ensuring the most impactful issues get resolved first.

Repair Verification

Authorities upload before-and-after repair photographs. Citizens can visually verify that work was completed properly before the complaint is closed.

The Complaint Lifecycle

1. Submitted

Citizen files a report with GPS pin, photo, category, and description. System generates a unique Reference ID (e.g. CIV-20240710-XXXXX). Duplicate detection runs automatically.

2. Verified & Assigned

Admin validates the complaint and the system auto-routes it to the appropriate municipal department. The officer is notified.

3. Under Review / Repair Started

Field inspection occurs. Work crew is dispatched. Status updates and "Before Repair" photos are uploaded to the complaint timeline.

4. Repair In Progress

Active remediation is underway. Citizens can monitor the live status update trail at any time using their Reference ID.

5. Resolved & Closed

Work is complete. "After Repair" photos are uploaded. A printable resolution receipt is generated. The complaint is archived.
