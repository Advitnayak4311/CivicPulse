Your privacy matters. CivicPulse collects only the minimum data necessary to deliver the Platform's services. We do not sell your personal data to any third party.

1. Overview

CivicPulse Municipal Corporation ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of all users of the CivicPulse Platform ("Platform"). This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how it is used, and the rights you have over your information.

This policy applies to all users including citizens who register accounts, anonymous users who track complaints publicly, and municipal authority users. By using the Platform, you consent to the data practices described herein.

2.3 Emergency Report Data

For emergency reports submitted without a registered account, we collect name, email address, and mobile number solely for OTP verification and complaint attribution. This data is linked to a minimal guest account that can be upgraded by the user upon registration.

2.4 Automatically Collected Data

We may automatically collect browser type, device type, IP address, and access timestamps for security monitoring and platform performance analysis. This data is not shared with third parties and is retained for a maximum of 30 days.

3. How We Use Your Data

  • Service Delivery: Process and route your complaints to the appropriate municipal department.
  • Authentication: Verify your identity during login and OTP verification.
  • Notifications: Send status updates on your complaints (email).
  • Platform Improvement: Analyse aggregated, anonymised usage data to improve features.
  • Legal Compliance: Respond to lawful government requests and comply with applicable Indian law.
  • Security: Detect and prevent fraudulent, abusive, or illegal activity.

We do not use your data for targeted advertising, profiling for commercial purposes, or any processing beyond what is described in this Policy.

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We share your data only with:

  • Municipal Department Officers: Your name, complaint details, GPS location, and contact info are shared with the assigned department officer to facilitate repair work.
  • System Administrators: CivicPulse admins can access all complaint data for moderation and oversight purposes.
  • Legal Authorities: We may disclose data when required by a court order, government directive, or applicable law under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

We never sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any commercial third party, advertising network, or data broker.

5. OTP & Emergency Report Data

OTP records generated for emergency report verification are stored temporarily with a maximum validity of 10 minutes. After expiry or successful use, OTP records are automatically purged from our database using MongoDB TTL (Time-to-Live) indexes. The associated email and phone number are retained only as part of the guest user account until the user deletes their account or requests data erasure.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • Password Hashing: All passwords are hashed using bcrypt (salt rounds: 10) before storage. Plaintext passwords are never stored or logged.
  • JWT Authentication: Session tokens use signed JSON Web Tokens with configurable expiry to prevent session hijacking.
  • HTTPS Enforcement: All production deployments enforce HTTPS. HTTP traffic is redirected.
  • Role-Based Access Control: Citizens, authorities, and admins have strictly scoped access — each role can only access data permitted to their level.
  • File Upload Restrictions: Uploaded files are validated for MIME type and size (max 5MB). Files are stored outside the web root.

Despite these measures, no digital transmission is 100% secure. We encourage users to use strong, unique passwords and report any suspicious activity immediately.

8. Your Rights

Under applicable Indian data protection law (including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023), you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your account and associated personal data (subject to legal retention obligations).
  • Withdrawal of Consent: Withdraw consent to data processing where processing is based on consent.
  • Grievance Redressal: Lodge a complaint with our designated Grievance Officer.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@civicpulse.gov.in. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Cookies & Local Storage

CivicPulse uses browser localStorage (not cookies) to store your authentication token and user session data locally. This data is stored only on your device and is cleared when you log out. We do not use third-party tracking cookies or analytics cookies.

10. Children's Privacy

The CivicPulse Platform is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you become aware that a child has provided us with personal data without parental consent, please contact us at privacy@civicpulse.gov.in and we will take steps to delete such information.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. When we make material changes, we will notify registered users via email and display a prominent notice on the Platform homepage for at least 30 days. The "Last Reviewed" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Your continued use of the Platform after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.
