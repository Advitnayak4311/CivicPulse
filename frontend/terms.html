2. Service Description

CivicPulse is a digital civic issue reporting and monitoring platform that enables:

  • Citizens to report municipal infrastructure problems (potholes, drainage failures, street light outages, etc.) with GPS-precise location and photographic evidence.
  • Municipal authorities to track, assign, and resolve reported complaints through a structured workflow.
  • The general public to track complaint status using a Reference ID, without requiring account registration.
  • Emergency issue reporting via an OTP-verified channel without prior registration.

CivicPulse is a supplementary civic engagement tool and does not replace official government helplines, emergency services, or existing grievance redressal systems mandated by law.

5. Complaint Submission Rules

When submitting a complaint, you agree to:

  • Report only genuine civic infrastructure issues within your municipal jurisdiction.
  • Provide accurate GPS coordinates and truthful descriptions of the problem.
  • Upload authentic photographic evidence only. Manipulated or fabricated images are strictly prohibited.
  • Not submit duplicate complaints for the same location and category. Use the "Support" feature to back existing reports instead.
  • Not use the platform to report private property disputes, inter-personal conflicts, or issues outside the platform's scope.

CivicPulse employs automatic duplicate detection within a 100-metre radius. Wilful repeated duplicate submissions may result in account suspension.

6. Emergency Report Channel

The Emergency Report channel is provided as a fast-track pathway for citizens facing critical civic hazards that pose immediate risk to life or property. By using this channel, you agree that:

  • You will use it only for genuine emergencies such as road cave-ins, burst water mains, exposed live wires, or similar hazards.
  • The OTP verification process is for identity confirmation only and does not constitute a contractual agreement.
  • False or frivolous emergency reports may result in your email and phone being flagged and blocked from future submissions.
  • For life-threatening emergencies (medical, fire, police), you must call 112 — this platform cannot dispatch emergency services.
